SWM Rope-Stretch

Designed and implemented a semi-automated rope feed system to replace the manual winding process on the paper machine, improving both efficiency and operator safety. The system featured a series of rollers and tensioners to ensure consistent rope feed, reducing downtime and labor intensity. Using AutoCAD, I developed detailed schematics and part diagrams for fabrication, collaborating closely with the maintenance team to integrate the system seamlessly with existing equipment. Throughout the project, I maintained thorough documentation, including BOMs, schematics, and operational procedures, to facilitate future maintenance and system improvements.

Jeep Restoration

Undertaking a complete restoration of a 1946 Willys Jeep CJ-2A, focusing on both mechanical and electrical components. The drivetrain was fully rebuilt, involving complete disassembly, replacement of worn parts, and reassembly to factory specifications. The body and frame restoration included repairing structural damage through welding, fabricating missing sections, and rebuilding the wiring harness to ensure proper electrical functionality.
